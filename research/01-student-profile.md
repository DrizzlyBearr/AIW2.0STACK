Pattern recognition:
- Founder-led businesses, founders build product and rough ICP then start selling without any infrastructure underneath; the bad habits are baked in by the time they seek help
- Legal intake, speed to lead and unqualified intake staff are the consistent failure points; the fix is well-defined and repeatable across firms of similar size
- Follow-up, most businesses sit on pipelines full of warm interest they never properly worked; this is a revenue recovery problem, not a lead generation problem
- Pitch scaling, a founder can close brilliantly one-on-one but the pitch only exists in their head; when a rep is put on it without a documented framework, conversion tanks and the rep gets blamed
- Pipeline visibility, most businesses cannot name their show rate, conversion rate per stage, or drop-off point; they call it a sales problem when it is a visibility problem
- Channel mismatch, businesses have a serviceable ICP but prospect in the wrong place because they default to familiar channels rather than where the buyer actually lives
