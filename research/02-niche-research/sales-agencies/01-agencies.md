# Sub-task 1: Agencies Serving Sales Agencies / Sales Consulting Firms
# Niche: Sales agencies / sales infrastructure businesses

Research date: 2026-05-21

---

## Overview

No agency currently dominates the narrow category of "website design for sales agencies." The market fragments into three supplier types: (1) generic B2B web design agencies that will take any professional services client, (2) boutique consultants who specialise in consulting firm websites but have no sales-specific IP, and (3) offshore/freelance generalists on Upwork and Fiverr who deliver templates with no niche understanding. None of the agencies found specifically positions itself as the go-to web partner for outsourced sales, fractional sales, or sales infrastructure companies.

## Agency Profiles

### 1. Bop Design
**URL:** https://www.bopdesign.com  
**What they do:** B2B web design and content marketing for professional services and technology companies. Consistently ranked among top B2B web design agencies. Produced a "Top 20 B2B website designs" list for 2024 that is widely cited.  
**Pros:** Deep B2B expertise, strong conversion-focused copy, case study depth, CRO-aware layouts.  
**Cons:** Generalist B2B positioning. No sales-agency-specific IP. No positioning around outsourced sales or fractional sales buyer psychology. Pricing not published but widely estimated at $15,000-$50,000+ for a custom engagement.  
**Approach:** Results and capability focused. Copy emphasises lead generation and qualified traffic rather than product features.  
**Trust elements:** Client logos, case study library, industry awards.  
**Gap left:** No niche templates, no copy IP around "founder-led sales transition" or "proving ROI before they pay." A sales agency client gets the same process as a logistics software firm.

### 2. Ironpaper
**URL:** https://www.ironpaper.com  
**What they do:** B2B website design and inbound lead generation exclusively for companies with long sales cycles. Founded 2003, ~70 specialists, headquartered in New York.  
**Pros:** B2B-only focus, deep expertise in lead gen mechanics, HubSpot integration, ongoing retainer model, documented case studies with pipeline metrics.  
**Cons:** Long sales cycle focus means they optimise for 6-12 month nurture journeys, not the short-cycle "book a call this week" pattern that sales agencies need. Pricing not published; estimated $10,000-$30,000 for initial build plus retainer.  
**Approach:** Demand generation, content strategy, SEO embedded from day one.  
**Trust elements:** Named client case studies with metrics, B2B-only positioning, HubSpot partner badge.  
**Gap left:** Their model assumes long cycles and marketing automation. Sales agencies need a site that converts cold traffic to booked calls in one visit. Ironpaper builds content engines, not immediate-conversion machines.

### 8. Freelance generalists (Upwork/Fiverr)
**URL:** https://www.upwork.com / https://www.fiverr.com  
**What they do:** Template-based or WordPress/Webflow builds. Available for $500-$5,000 for a full site.

## Top 3 Patterns Winners Share

1. **Results before features.** The best agencies in this space lead with client outcomes (revenue generated, pipeline built, meetings booked) before explaining what services they offer. Ironpaper, Bop Design, and 310 Creative all lead with impact, not capability.

2. **Named social proof.** Winners show real company logos, real case study numbers, and real industry context. Generic "45+ happy clients" does not convert. Named clients in recognisable verticals convert.

3. **A specific buyer in the headline.** Top performers write for one person. "For B2B tech companies stuck in founder-led sales" outperforms "B2B web design for professional services." Boutique Growth and 310 Creative are closest to this.

---

## Top 3 Patterns Losers Share

1. **Feature inventory pages.** Losers list services (web design, SEO, social media, branding) in equal-weight bullet points. No hierarchy, no primary outcome, no reason to care.

2. **Generic trust signals.** Stock photos of handshakes, vague "years of experience" stats, no client names, no revenue numbers. Signals effort, not results.

3. **No niche specificity.** Losers could swap the name of any industry onto their homepage and it would make equal sense. "We build websites that convert" for roofers, lawyers, and sales agencies alike. Buyers in high-trust B2B categories want to see that the agency has been here before.
