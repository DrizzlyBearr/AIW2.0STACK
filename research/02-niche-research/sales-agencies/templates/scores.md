### Distinctive moves

1. **Authority-first hero positioning.** The "#1 B2B Lead Generation Agency" claim in the H1 primes the entire page -- the buyer immediately frames everything that follows as proof of an established leader, not an underdog asking for a chance.
2. **Metric-specific case studies.** Named percentage improvements (150% booked meetings) placed early in the page structure convert better than generic logos because they answer the buyer's implicit question: "will this actually move my numbers?"

### Anti-patterns

1. **Problem/Agitate section absent.** The research explicitly notes Belkins skips the problem-agitation block, assuming buyer awareness. 2. **No risk-reversal section.** No "what happens if we don't deliver" guarantee or SLA language is present, which is the #1 missed conversion opportunity in the category.
3. **Social proof is logo-heavy, not quote-heavy.** Logos without named executive quotes are weaker trust signals than "Here is what the CMO of [Company] said after 90 days" -- the research confirms this gap exists for Belkins.

## Cross-site pattern synthesis (for template use)

### What the top 3 share that the bottom 5 do not

1. **Outcome-in-hero, not category-in-hero.** Belkins ("#1 B2B Lead Generation Agency"), Martal (outcome-led with audience specificity), and CIENCE (category authority) all lead with a statement the buyer wants to be true. The bottom five lead with what the company does.
2. **Four-layer trust stacking.** Stats > Logos > Review badges > Named quotes. Sites that skip or reorder layers -- especially sites that lead with tech logos before human proof -- score lower across the board.
3. **Explicit process section.** Every top-3 site has a "How It Works" or equivalent 3-4 step sequence. This reduces perceived risk by making the engagement feel predictable before the buyer has committed.

### Universal gaps across all 8 candidates (template opportunities)

1. **No risk-reversal section on any site.** A "What happens if we miss our targets" section with specific guarantee language is absent across all eight candidates -- this is the highest-value differentiator the factory template can build in.
2. **Case studies are all short-form.** None of the candidates has a narrative case study (problem > approach > result > client reflection). The factory template should include a long-form case study section structure even if it is initially placeholder.
3. **Objection handling is FAQ-buried.** Only Martal has an explicit build-vs-buy section. The factory template should surface the top three buyer objections (will you understand my market / will I get locked in to a long contract / what happens if results are slow) as a dedicated pre-CTA section rather than hiding them in a footer FAQ.
4. **No named quotes beside logos.** Every site uses logos-only in the social proof bar. Pairing a one-line named quote with the logo ("'30 meetings in 60 days' -- VP Sales, Acme Corp") is a high-value improvement the template can lead with.
