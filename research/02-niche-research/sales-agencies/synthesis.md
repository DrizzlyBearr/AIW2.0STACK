## End customer in one sentence

A founder or SMB owner with a product that works and a revenue problem that does not -- stuck doing every sales call themselves, one failed sales hire away from their next bad decision, looking for someone who can build the function so they can stop being the function.

## The student's client in one sentence

A sales agency or sales infrastructure business (typically 2-10 people, $500K-$3M ARR, founder still deeply embedded in delivery) that closes deals through relationships and referrals today, has no website that converts cold traffic, and needs a credibility asset that can support outbound outreach and inbound discovery.

---

## Top 5 Insights

**1. The buyer's primary fear is "they won't understand my business" -- and most websites don't address it.**
The top buyer objection across Clutch reviews, agency FAQs, and founder editorial content is not price or contract length. It is the fear that an external sales team will misrepresent the business to prospects. Winning websites address this directly and early: industry-specific case studies, market-understanding language in the hero, and named quotes from clients whose business type matches the visitor's. Most current sales agency websites bury this in a FAQ. A purpose-built template can make it a section.

**2. There is no agency that specialises in building websites for sales agencies or sales infrastructure companies.**
The market gaps confirmed in Sub-task 1: generic B2B agencies serve anyone, niche consulting-website designers focus on strategy and content, and offshore freelancers deliver templates. No agency has sales-agency-specific IP: copy frameworks about founder-led transition pain, ROI math built for the buyer of outsourced sales, trust architectures tuned to the "will you deliver?" fear. Katleho enters as the only builder who has lived this category from the inside.

**3. Case studies with specific numbers convert; everything else is noise.**
Every data point from Sub-tasks 2, 3, and 5 confirms this. Buyers in this category have been burned and are sophisticated. "We increased revenue" is dismissed. "MRR from $80K to $162K in 4 months at $12K/month -- ROI positive by month 4" gets read. The website factory must include a case study section template that forces a specific number, a timeframe, and a client context. Sales agency clients who cannot provide one case study with real numbers should be told to come back when they can.

**4. The founder-led sales transition is the highest-intent entry point for content and copy.**
The queries "how to transition from founder-led sales," "when to hire first sales rep," and "sales playbook before hiring" are searched by buyers who are at the exact moment of deciding to get help. Current ranking content for these queries is editorial (SaaStr, Kellblog, Growth Unhinged), not from sales agencies themselves. A sales infrastructure company with 3-5 strong articles on this topic can rank and pull in buyers who never searched "outsourced sales team." This is the primary SEO content opportunity.

**5. Pricing transparency is a differentiator, not a risk.**
RevBoss publishes pricing starting at $2,500/month. They convert price-sensitive buyers faster than agencies that hide pricing. Most sales agencies do not publish pricing and make buyers jump through a discovery call to find out if they can afford the service. A "typical engagement starts at X" note on the website reduces bounce rate among qualified buyers who are testing affordability before they book.

## Top 3 Risks If We Pick This Niche

- **Katleho's positioning as a website builder will be questioned.** Sales infrastructure businesses are sophisticated buyers who evaluate credibility intensely. Katleho building their website is credible because he runs one. But "sales infrastructure company hires a web developer to build their website" is still a consultative sell, not a productised one. The pitch needs to lean on Katleho's insider knowledge, not just design skills.

- **High expectation, hard to fake results.** Sales agency clients will expect the website to generate inbound leads. If the website does not convert, the client will not renew or refer. The factory must output something that genuinely works, not something that looks good. The pressure on quality is higher than in more outcome-ambiguous niches (like law firms or home services).

- **Category is dominated by well-funded players with strong domain authority.** Belkins, CIENCE, Martal, and SalesHive have spent years building SEO authority. A new website in this space will not rank organically for primary keywords without 12-24 months of content investment. The immediate value is inbound credibility (when a prospect is referred and checks the site), not organic search acquisition.
