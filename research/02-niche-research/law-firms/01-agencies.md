# 01 - Agencies: Law Firm Website Design

Research date: 2026-05-21
Niche: Small to mid-size law firms (1-20 attorneys)

## Agency Profiles

### 1. PaperStreet Web Design
**URL:** https://www.paperstreet.com
**Founded:** 2001 | Founded by a lawyer
**Portfolio:** ~1,500+ law firm websites built

**Approach:** Custom design-first. Heavy emphasis on visual quality, award-winning aesthetics, SEO as a secondary layer. Content written by attorney-informed writers. Exclusivity: they do not serve competing firms in the same market.

**Pricing:**
- Essentials: ~$3,000 total first-year cost (template-based, basic SEO)
- Plus: $9,500 to $15,000+
- Custom: $15,000 to $25,000+
- Enterprise (AmLaw 100/500): $40,000+
- Monthly maintenance sold separately

**Pros:**
- Deep legal industry experience, 25+ years
- Visually polished output, consistent 5-star ratings from clients
- Exclusivity protects client from competitor overlap
- Comprehensive SEO: on-page, technical, local
- Attorney-reviewed copy

**Cons:**
- Expensive relative to alternatives at every tier
- Pricing lacks full transparency (not on main site, buried in blog)
- Custom tier prices out most small firms under $15k budget
- Focus is more on appearance and SEO than on intake conversion mechanics
- No published data on conversion rate outcomes for clients

**Trust signals emphasized:** Bar association logos, Super Lawyers, awards, case results, attorney headshots, testimonials

**Gaps left:** No intake pipeline integration or speed-to-lead tooling. Strong on visual trust, weak on post-form-submit conversion. Pricing locks out solo and 2-3 attorney firms.

## Top 3 Patterns Winners Share

1. **Legal exclusivity.** Every agency that performs well works only with law firms (or only with attorneys). General web agencies cannot replicate 20 years of legal-specific conversion knowledge, bar compliance familiarity, and E-E-A-T content standards.

2. **Attorney-authored or attorney-reviewed content.** The top agencies (PaperStreet, LawRank, JurisPage, NextClient) all deploy lawyers or JD holders to write or review copy. Legal YMYL content demands this for both bar compliance and Google trust signals.

3. **Owned, portable WordPress builds.** The most trusted agencies deliver sites the firm owns outright, on WordPress, with full portability. Firms burned by Scorpion's CMS-8 lock-in actively seek this guarantee.

---

## Top 3 Patterns Losers Share

1. **Proprietary platform lock-in.** Scorpion's CMS-8 is the clearest example. When the firm wants to leave, they must rebuild from zero. This is a trust-destroying practice that generates negative reviews and churn.

2. **Design-only focus with no conversion architecture.** Many agencies deliver beautiful sites with no intake form optimization, no speed-to-lead tooling, no live chat, and no multi-step form strategy. The average law firm website converts at 2-4%. Top performers hit 8-15%. The gap is almost entirely in conversion mechanics, not aesthetics.

3. **Opacity on pricing and results.** Agencies that hide pricing, obscure ad spend vs. management fee splits, or cannot show documented conversion improvements lose credibility with law firm buyers who are trained to scrutinize contracts. Transparency is a competitive advantage that losers forfeit.
