# 04 - CRO Patterns: Page Structure and Conversion Mechanics

Research date: 2026-05-21
Niche: Small to mid-size law firms (1-20 attorneys)
End customer: Person in crisis searching for legal help

---

## Conversion Benchmarks

- Average law firm website conversion rate: 2-4% of visitors
- Median legal landing page: 6.3% conversion
- Top performers (optimized law firm sites): 8-15%
- Paid search (high-intent): 8.3% median
- Personal injury and criminal defense pages with strong urgency: often 10%+
- Bankruptcy and tax pages: convert above 13% (extreme urgency)
- General practice pages: often below 3%
- Mobile traffic converts 30-50% lower than desktop for unoptimized sites
- Top third of law firms average ~100 leads/month; bottom third average ~22 leads/month
- Source: LEXGRO, Epic Attorney Marketing, Legal Brand Marketing research aggregation

## Site 1: Morgan & Morgan (forthepeople.com)
National PI firm. Benchmark for conversion architecture.

**Section order (top to bottom):**
1. Sticky navigation: phone number + "Get a Free Case Evaluation" button (always visible)
2. Hero: "For the People" -- full-screen image of attorney, bold headline, CTA button center screen
3. Practice area grid: injury types (car accident, slip and fall, medical malpractice, etc.) -- each links to dedicated page
4. Case results ticker: scrolling dollar amounts ($4.1M, $2.3M, etc.)
5. "Tell us about your situation" free case evaluation form -- 3-4 fields: name, phone, case type, brief description
6. Video testimonials section
7. Attorney team photos
8. Awards and recognition bar: Super Lawyers, verdicts, media logos
9. FAQ accordion
10. Footer CTA: phone number + form repeat

**Hero composition:** Full-screen photo of attorney (warm, confident expression), firm's brand color accent, headline centered with CTA button directly beneath. No distractions. One action.

**Trust stack ordering:** Outcome proof (case results) before testimonials before credentials. Results first because they answer "can you win?" before "are you trustworthy?"

**Form pattern:** 3-4 fields maximum. Name, phone, case type dropdown, brief description. Single-step. No required fields beyond name and phone. Submit button: "Get Your Free Evaluation."

**Sticky elements:** Phone number in header (sticky), mobile sticky bottom bar with click-to-call and "chat now" options.

## Site 2: Zaner Harden Law (zanerhardenlaw.com)
Denver personal injury. Known for emotional + credibility balance.

**Section order:**
1. Sticky header: phone, "Free Consultation" button
2. Hero: full-screen image of attorneys with clients/families, headline "Fighting for the Seriously Injured in Colorado"
3. Social proof bar: multimillion-dollar verdicts and settlements prominently noted
4. Practice areas: car accidents, truck accidents, medical malpractice, etc.
5. Case results with dollar amounts
6. Client testimonials (video + text)
7. Attorney team bios with photos
8. Awards bar: Super Lawyers, Best Lawyers in America, AV Preeminent
9. Contact form: name, phone, email, case description -- 4 fields
10. Footer CTA

**Hero composition:** Attorneys shown interacting kindly with clients -- not posed courthouse steps. Signals approachability. "Seriously injured" in headline signals case-type selection (high-value cases only).

**Trust stack ordering:** Outcomes (verdicts) immediately below hero, then testimonials, then attorney credentials. Same pattern as Morgan & Morgan: prove you win before proving you're nice.

**Key divergence from generic wireframe:** No "problem/agitate" section. Law firm sites skip the classic copywriting agitation step because the prospect has already been agitated by real life. They arrive pre-agitated. The site's job is to calm and convert.

## Site 3: Razumich & Associates (Criminal Defense)
Indiana criminal defense. High-intent, fear-driven audience.

**Section order:**
1. Sticky header: phone number prominent, dark color scheme (power, authority)
2. Hero: "Lawyers Ready to Fight" -- no softness, no photos of family. Power imagery.
3. Practice areas: DUI, drug charges, felonies, federal crimes, domestic violence
4. Why choose us: experience stats (years, cases handled, not guilty verdicts)
5. Attorney bios with credentials
6. Client testimonials
7. Free consultation CTA section
8. FAQ: "What happens after I'm charged?" type questions
9. Footer with phone number

**Hero composition:** Dark background, attorney in suit, confident posture. Zero family imagery. Prospect is scared and wants a warrior, not a counselor.

**Trust stack ordering:** Experience and results before testimonials. "Not guilty" count matters more than warmth. Case results in criminal defense are stated as outcomes (charges dropped, acquitted, reduced) not dollar amounts.

**Form pattern:** 3 fields only -- name, phone, type of charge. "Call now is better than a form" is the implicit design choice. Phone number displayed larger than the form.

**Divergence from wireframe:** No "How it works" section -- criminal defense clients do not want a process overview; they want immediate reassurance. The "objection handling" section is replaced by FAQ addressing arrest fears.

## Site 4: Modern Law (Divorce/Family Law)
Clean modern design, conversion-optimized for divorce clients.

**Section order:**
1. Sticky header: "Talk to us now" and "Book an appointment" buttons visible at all times
2. Hero: Bold photo, contrasting colors, headline communicating divorce specialization
3. Two primary CTAs in hero: "Talk to us now" (phone) + "Book an appointment" (form)
4. Practice areas: divorce, child custody, property division, spousal support
5. "What our clients say" -- testimonials section
6. Attorney profiles
7. Process overview ("How we work with you") -- family law clients want reassurance about the process
8. FAQ section: "How long does a divorce take?" "What will it cost?"
9. Footer CTA

**Hero composition:** Dual CTA placement directly in hero is a divorce-specific pattern. Divorce clients are more deliberate (not in same acute emergency as PI or criminal). They want to schedule, not just call. Two CTAs serve two intent levels: "ready now" and "not quite ready."

**Trust stack ordering:** Testimonials higher in the page than credentials. Divorce clients respond more to "someone like me got through this" than to legal awards.

**Divergence from wireframe:** "How it works" section is present and prominent for family law (unlike criminal defense). Process transparency reduces fear about the unknown length and cost of divorce. This is the one practice area where process content converts.

## Universal Wireframe Anchoring and Law Firm Divergences

**Standard wireframe:** Hero > Social proof bar > Problem/agitate > Solution > How it works > Case studies > Objection handling > CTA > FAQ > Final CTA

**Law firm divergences:**

| Section | Law Firm Pattern | Why Different |
|---|---|---|
| Problem/Agitate | Usually absent | Client arrives already in crisis. Agitation is counterproductive. |
| Solution | Replaced by "practice areas" | Clients need to confirm you handle their specific problem. |
| How It Works | Present for family law/estate only; absent for PI/criminal | PI and criminal clients want action, not process education. |
| Case studies | "Case results" with dollar amounts or charge outcomes | Specific numbers > narrative stories |
| Social proof bar | Placed immediately below hero, not further down | Trust must be established within 3 seconds of landing |
| CTA | Repeated 3-5 times throughout page | Legal buying decisions are high-stakes; multiple CTA exposures required |
| Objection handling | Replaced by FAQ | "Will this cost me?" and "Do I have a case?" are the objections |

## Form Patterns

- Field count: 3-4 fields is the optimal range for law firm contact forms
- Fields that convert: Name + Phone + Case Type + Brief Description
- Required vs optional: Name and Phone required. Email optional. Description optional but helpful.
- Multi-step forms: Convert better than single long-form when firm wants more detail. Step 1 (Name, Phone) gets the lead. Step 2 (case details) qualifies it.
- Best submit button copy: "Get My Free Case Review" | "Find Out If I Have a Case" | "Start My Consultation"
- Worst submit button copy: "Submit" | "Send" | "Contact Us"
- Placement: In hero (highest conversion), above fold in sidebar, and repeated in page middle and footer

---

## Sticky Elements

**Desktop:** Sticky navigation bar with phone number always visible. Scroll-triggered "free consultation" banner.

**Mobile:** Bottom sticky bar is the single highest-impact conversion element for law firm sites. Pattern: left side = click-to-call button | right side = "Chat" or "Text us" button. This removes all friction for the mobile visitor who is one tap away from a conversion.

**Chat widgets:** Live chat during business hours with after-hours chatbot. Captures after-hours criminal defense and PI leads (accidents, arrests happen at night and on weekends). Juvo Leads research shows multi-channel approach (chat + call + text) significantly increases total leads from mobile.
