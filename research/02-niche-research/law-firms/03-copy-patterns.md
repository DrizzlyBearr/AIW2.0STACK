# 03 - Copy Patterns: Hero Copy, CTAs, and Value Props

Research date: 2026-05-21
Niche: Small to mid-size law firms (1-20 attorneys)
End customer: Person in crisis searching for legal help

---

## Top 10 Hero Headlines

Real and research-confirmed headlines used by high-profile law firm websites. Pattern notes follow each.

1. **"For the People"** -- Morgan & Morgan (forthepeople.com)
   Pattern: Brand-as-mission positioning. Communicates advocacy by the name itself. Works at scale with massive brand recognition but also works as a concept for smaller firms.

2. **"Injured? We Fight for You."** -- Multiple PI firms
   Pattern: Problem-solution in 5 words. Opens with the client's state (injured), immediately answers with action (fight for you). The most common structural pattern in PI.

3. **"Fighting for the Seriously Injured in Colorado"** -- Zaner Harden Law variant
   Pattern: Specificity + geography + advocacy. "Seriously injured" selects for high-value cases. Geographic specificity builds local relevance signal.

4. **"You pay nothing unless we win."** -- Inland Empire Injury Law and many PI firms
   Pattern: Risk reversal as hero headline. Eliminates the largest barrier to contact (cost fear). Works because it answers the prospect's unstated objection before they leave.

5. **"A Team of Attorneys Ready to Fight For You"** -- Price Benowitz
   Pattern: Team framing + advocacy. "Team" signals depth and capacity. "Fight for you" is the universal PI emotional hook.

6. **"Lawyers Ready to Fight"** -- Razumich & Associates
   Pattern: Criminal defense variant. Short, declarative, aggressive. Matches the emotional need of someone facing charges -- they want aggression, not warmth.

7. **"Free Consultation. No Fee Unless We Win."** -- Multiple contingency firms
   Pattern: Double risk-reversal. Combines both main barriers (cost of the consult, cost of the case) into a single promise. Common in PI, increasingly used in criminal and family.

8. **"Millions Recovered for Our Clients"** -- Multiple PI firm hero subheadlines
   Pattern: Outcome proof as hero element. Used more often as subheadline beneath an emotional headline. Quantifies past performance to address the "will they win?" fear.

9. **"In a wreck? Need a check?"** -- Lerner and Rowe
   Pattern: Rhyme/memorability. High-volume TV advertising firm. Works in TV/radio where frequency drives recall. Less effective in digital where emotional trust matters more.

10. **"If you've been hurt, we're here to help. If life hasn't been fair to you, we're here to fight for the underdog."** -- TopDog Law
    Pattern: Empathy-first, dual message. Acknowledges both the physical harm and the emotional injustice. "Underdog" creates identification for working-class clients.

## Top 10 CTAs

Listed with pattern notes. Source: research across agency analyses, CTA-focused legal marketing studies, and specific firm sites.

1. **"Get a Free Case Evaluation"** -- Most common CTA in personal injury
   Pattern: Free + specific deliverable. "Evaluation" sounds more substantive than "consultation."

2. **"Call Now for a Free Consultation"** -- Universal across all practice areas
   Pattern: Imperative + free + immediacy. Most overused but still effective because of clarity.

3. **"Schedule Your Free 30-Minute Consultation"** -- JurisPage clients and similar
   Pattern: Specificity converts. "30-minute" sets an expectation, reduces commitment fear.

4. **"Find Out What Your Case Is Worth"** -- High-converting PI variant
   Pattern: Curiosity + self-interest. The prospect's core question answered. Drives higher intent clicks than generic "contact us."

5. **"Speak with an Attorney Today"** -- Emphasizes direct attorney access
   Pattern: Addresses fear of being handed to a paralegal. "Today" creates urgency.

6. **"Tell Us What Happened"** -- Morgan & Morgan style
   Pattern: Narrative invitation. Low-commitment language. Reduces the psychological barrier of "contacting a lawyer" to simply "telling someone your story."

7. **"Click to Get Help Now"** -- Criminal defense variant
   Pattern: Emergency language. Works for criminal defense where the crisis is acute and time-sensitive.

8. **"No Fee Unless We Win -- Call Today"** -- Risk reversal + action
   Pattern: Combines the main objection handler with a direct action. Works as both headline and CTA.

9. **"Start Your Free Consultation"** -- Action verb variation
   Pattern: "Start" is more active than "schedule." Implies forward motion, not a passive event.

10. **"See If You Have a Case"** -- Low-commitment qualifier CTA
    Pattern: Removes implied pressure of "hiring" a lawyer. Invites self-qualification. Good for fence-sitters.

## Top 10 Value Props

Used in hero subheadlines, trust bars, and above-the-fold copy sections.

1. **"No fee unless we win"** -- Universal PI. Eliminates cost barrier.

2. **"Available 24/7"** -- Addresses the after-hours emergency caller. Common in criminal defense and PI.

3. **"Over $X million / billion recovered"** -- Quantified outcome proof. Varies by firm size ($5M for small regional to $1B+ for Morgan & Morgan).

4. **"Free consultation"** -- Universal. Removes the initial cost objection.

5. **"40+ years of combined experience"** -- Experience as a shortcut for trust. Specific numbers outperform vague "years of experience."

6. **"Local attorneys who know [City] courts"** -- Geographic authority. Clients prefer attorneys who know local judges and procedures.

7. **"We respond within [X hours / minutes]"** -- Speed-to-lead promise. Increasingly used as a differentiator as the industry average is 42 hours.

8. **"5-star rated on Google"** -- Third-party social proof. Google stars are the most trusted external rating in consumer research.

9. **"Serving [City/Region] since [Year]"** -- Longevity + geography. Signals stability, local roots.

10. **"You focus on recovery. We handle everything else."** -- Reframing the burden. Speaks directly to the exhausted injury client who doesn't want to manage their own case.

## Common Ad Hook Structures (Google / Facebook)

From research on high-performing personal injury law firm digital ads:

**Hook Structure 1: Problem + Aggressor + Rescue**
"[Insurance companies / negligent drivers / employers] don't play fair. We do. Call [Firm] for a free consultation."

**Hook Structure 2: Symptom + Diagnosis + Action**
"Neck pain after a car accident? You may be entitled to compensation. Call now for a free case review."

**Hook Structure 3: Benefit Lead**
"Most accident victims leave money on the table. Our attorneys typically recover 3-4x what insurance offers. See what your case is worth."

**Hook Structure 4: Fear removal**
"Worried about legal fees? At [Firm], you pay nothing until we win. Call for a free evaluation."

**Hook Structure 5: Social proof + invitation**
"Over 500 five-star reviews. Join thousands of clients who trusted [Firm] with their case. Free consultation, no obligation."

## 5 Swipeable Hero Headline Templates

Templates Katleho can use for any small-to-mid-size law firm website:

**Template 1: Problem + Advocacy**
"[Injured / Charged / Going through a divorce]? We Fight to Get You What You Deserve."
Use for: Personal injury, criminal defense, family law

**Template 2: Geography + Outcome**
"[City] [Practice Area] Lawyers Who [Won $X / Get Results / Know the Courts]."
Example: "Phoenix Personal Injury Lawyers Who Have Won Over $20 Million for Local Clients."

**Template 3: Risk Reversal + Action**
"Your Consultation Is Free. You Pay Nothing Unless We Win."
Use for: Personal injury, contingency-based practices

**Template 4: Empathy + Competence**
"You're going through one of the hardest times in your life. We handle the legal fight so you don't have to."
Use for: Divorce, serious injury, estate matters

**Template 5: Specificity + Local Authority**
"[X] Years Protecting [City] Families from [Insurance Companies / The Courts / Unfair Outcomes]."
Example: "22 Years Protecting Houston Families from Insurance Companies That Don't Pay What They Owe."
