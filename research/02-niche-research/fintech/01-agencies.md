### 1. Clay Global
URL: https://clay.global/fintech

Specialization: UI/UX, branding, and digital product design for fintech startups and enterprise organizations. Portfolio includes Coinbase, Credit Karma, and Stripe design systems.

Approach: Design-system-driven. Every project is built for scale, with high-fidelity prototyping and interaction design. Strong visual craft is the lead differentiator.

Pros:
- Senior team only; no juniors handling client work
- Deep fintech portfolio (Stripe, Coinbase, Credit Karma, GitHub)
- Builds scalable design systems, not one-off websites
- Founder-led team with strategic depth

Cons:
- Priced for $100K+ MRR companies ($50K to $150K per engagement)
- Design-led, not conversion-led; CRO thinking is secondary to craft
- No African market experience visible in portfolio
- Long timelines; not suited for early-stage startups needing speed

Trust elements emphasized: Premium portfolio logos, design system depth, Behance/awards presence.

Pricing: Custom; typical range $50,000 to $150,000. Time-and-materials, fixed-fee, or retainer options.

Gap left: Does not build for emerging market regulatory frameworks (FSCA, CBN, RBI). No local-market trust signals. CRO strategy is an afterthought versus craft.

## Top 3 Patterns Winners Share

1. Vertical depth beats breadth. CSTMR and Goodface win because they know the fintech vocabulary, the regulatory environment, and the trust signals that matter before the first client call. Generalists who add "fintech" to their service list cannot replicate this.

2. Conversion evidence is the differentiator. The agencies with the strongest positioning publish outcomes: onboarding completion rates, conversion lifts, leads generated. Agencies that only show pretty screenshots lose to those who show what the website did for revenue.

3. Compliance as a feature, not a footnote. Winning agencies frame compliance and regulation (FCA, PCI DSS, SOC 2) as conversion assets to be displayed strategically near CTAs, not buried in footers. They help clients turn "we are licensed" into trust-building copy.

---

## Top 3 Patterns Losers Share

1. Design-first, conversion-never. Most agencies lead with portfolio aesthetics. When the fintech website looks good but the CTA is buried, the form has 11 fields, and the regulatory credentials are in grey text in the footer, the site fails its business purpose regardless of how many awards it wins.

2. US/UK template applied globally. Every agency in this list prices, positions, and designs for a US or UK fintech company. None address FSCA registration, CBN licensing, African mobile money trust norms, or the specific fears of an African SMB evaluating a payment gateway. This is the gap Katleho can own.

3. One-time build, zero ongoing conversion thinking. Most agencies deliver a website and walk away. They do not provide A/B testing frameworks, conversion rate benchmarks, or post-launch optimization. The website becomes stale within 12 months of delivery.
