## 10 Most-Repeated Language Patterns

These are the phrases and concepts users reach for repeatedly when talking about fintech products.

1. "Hidden fees" -- The single most common trigger for abandoning a fintech product or switching. Used when fees are not surfaced in the hero or pricing page.

2. "Is it safe?" / "Is my money safe?" -- The first question for any new fintech user. Answered by regulatory credentials, security badges, and bank-level language.

3. "Support doesn't respond" / "I can't reach anyone" -- Top complaint driving churn. Users want human access, especially when something goes wrong.

4. "My funds are held" / "Account frozen" / "I can't access my money" -- The catastrophic fear event that drives people away from neobanks and fintech platforms.

5. "Too many steps" / "Too complicated to sign up" -- Signup friction is the conversion killer. Users expect an onboarding faster than opening a traditional bank account.

6. "I switched because of fees" -- Fee transparency and comparison is a primary acquisition driver. Users who left a competitor cite fees first.

7. "Regulated" / "Licensed" / "FCA" / "FSCA" / "CBN" -- Users search for and respond to regulatory credentials as the minimum bar for trust.

8. "Verification delays" / "KYC took forever" -- KYC is necessary but frustrating. Platforms that streamline it get praised; platforms that let it drag lose users.

9. "Works on mobile" / "Easy on my phone" -- African and global fintech users increasingly evaluate products on mobile-first UX quality.

10. "Limits" -- Users want to know what they cannot do before they commit. Transfer limits, withdrawal limits, and transaction limits are frequently mentioned complaints.

## Decision Moments

The specific events that push a user to sign up or switch:

1. Fee frustration at a traditional bank or competitor. A user receives a charge they did not expect and immediately opens a search tab looking for alternatives.

2. A friend or colleague recommends a platform by name. Referral is the dominant acquisition channel for consumer fintech. Social proof in messaging activates this.

3. A specific need arises: international transfer, cross-border payment, a trading account. The user searches for the best tool for that task and evaluates 2-3 options.

4. A business bottleneck. An SMB owner cannot collect payment in a new market and needs a gateway that supports their country.

5. A frustrating onboarding experience elsewhere. A competitor's 9-minute signup drives the user to look for something faster.

6. A compliance-driven switch. A platform drops support for unregistered businesses (as Paystack did at one point), forcing the business to move to a competitor mid-operation.

7. Seeing a specific metric that removes risk: "10 million users", "licensed by FCA", "$1B in transactions processed." A single trust number can tip a wavering prospect.

## Top 5 Fears When Choosing a Fintech Product

Listed in priority order based on frequency across all sources.

1. Losing access to their money. The Synapse collapse in 2024 (thousands of users locked out), the neobank frozen account epidemic, and Africa's cybercrime losses have made this the dominant fear. Users need to see credible answers to "What happens to my money if something goes wrong?"

2. Being scammed or defrauded. Users conflate legitimate fintech platforms with the scam platforms that promise high returns and disappear. Any new fintech faces an ambient association with fraud. Regulatory licensing, press coverage, and verifiable client counts are the antidote.

3. Hidden or undisclosed fees. Fintech's value proposition is often cheaper than banks. When fees appear that were not disclosed upfront, users feel deceived. This is particularly acute for African users dealing with currency conversion costs and local withdrawal fees.

4. Their data being sold or misused. Fintech handles more personal financial data than almost any other product category. Users are increasingly aware of this and look for clear data handling policies in plain language.

5. Not being able to reach a human when something goes wrong. This is particularly strong for B2B customers (SMBs) who need resolution within hours, not days. "I had no one to call" is a recurring complaint across every fintech Trustpilot page.
