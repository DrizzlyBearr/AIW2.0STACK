## Average LTV per Customer by Fintech Subsector

LTV figures drawn from published benchmarks: jahandarpour.com Series A LTV benchmarks, financialrhythms.com fintech unit economics, visora.co CAC metrics, ibinterviewquestions.com fintech valuation data.

### Median LTV: FinTech Series A
Published benchmark: $11,067 median LTV for Series A fintech companies.
Source: jahandarpour.com/benchmarks/ltv-fintech-series-a

### Payments Platform (B2B, SMB-focused like Paystack / Flutterwave)
- Revenue model: Transaction fee (1.5-2.9% per transaction) + monthly subscription for premium features
- Typical monthly revenue per SMB client: $50-$200/month in transaction fees
- Average contract duration: 2-4 years (stickiness is high because switching payment gateways requires developer work)
- Estimated LTV: $1,200 - $9,600 per SMB client
- Note: High-volume merchants (e.g., e-commerce brands doing $50K+/month) can contribute $5,000-$20,000+/year in fees

### Trading Platform (retail, education-first like TD Markets)
- Revenue model: Spread, commission per trade, subscription to education content, premium account tiers
- Typical monthly revenue per active trader: $30-$150/month depending on activity level
- Average active retention: 18-30 months
- Estimated LTV: $540 - $4,500 per active retail trader
- Note: The top 20% of active traders generate 80% of revenue. LTV is highly skewed.

### Subscription Fintech (like Thutsé -- subscription finance management)
- Revenue model: Monthly subscription fee ($10-$50/user/month)
- Average subscription churn: 3-8% monthly for consumer fintech; 1-3% for B2B fintech
- At $30/month with 5% monthly churn: LTV = $30 / 0.05 = $600
- At $30/month with 2% monthly churn (B2B): LTV = $30 / 0.02 = $1,500
- Estimated LTV range: $600 - $3,000 per subscriber

### Neobank / Digital Bank
- Accenture 2025 data: Average ARPU at a neobank = $45/year
- Traditional bank comparison: $350/year per customer
- Neobank LTV at $45/year over 5 years (50% retention): approximately $225 base
- However: cross-sell increases this 10x. A Chime-model customer who adds direct deposit + credit builder + savings = $40-$60/year. Over 5 years = $200-$300 in a conservative scenario; $800-$1,200 at maturity.
- The LTV story for neobanks is: low at acquisition, high at maturity. The website must communicate the full product journey, not just the entry product.

### Lending / Credit Fintech
- Revenue model: Origination fees + interest margin
- Typical origination fee: 2-6% of loan amount
- Average loan size (SMB fintech lending): $5,000 - $50,000
- Origination revenue per loan: $100 - $3,000
- Repeat loan rate for satisfied borrowers: 40-60%
- Estimated LTV with repeat usage: $1,000 - $15,000 depending on loan size and frequency

## Estimated Conversion Rate for a Well-Designed Fintech Landing Page

Source: Unbounce Q4 2024 Benchmark Report (57 million conversions analyzed, 41,000 landing pages).

- Financial services median conversion rate: 8.4%
- Cross-industry average: 6.6%
- Top quartile fintech pages: 12-15%

Fintech-specific conversion boosters:
- Reducing signup from 7+ fields to email + password: conversion from 18% to 54% (one crypto exchange case study)
- Adding regulatory badge near CTA: 10-20% conversion lift
- Eliminating 24 non-value-adding onboarding steps: 145% conversion lift (one bank case study)
- Adding social proof (live activity feed): conversion from 2% to 11% (one payment platform case study)

Working assumption for Katleho's fintech clients:
- A poorly built fintech website (generic template, no trust signals, cluttered form): 2-3% conversion rate
- A well-built, conversion-optimized fintech website: 8-12% conversion rate

The website is the difference between 2.5% and 10% conversion. On 1,000 monthly visitors, that is 25 signups versus 100 signups. At a $1,200 LTV per SMB client (payments platform), that is a difference of $90,000 in revenue from the same traffic.

## Leads / Signups Needed Per Month for Meaningful Revenue

This section answers the question for a fintech founder: how many signups does the website need to generate to justify the investment?

### Scenario: Payments Platform (B2B SMB)
- Target: $20,000/month in additional revenue
- Average LTV per SMB client: $4,800 (using midpoint of $1,200-$9,600 range; 24-month average)
- Monthly revenue contribution of each new client: $4,800 / 24 = $200/month
- New clients needed per month: $20,000 / $200 = 100 new clients
- At 10% website conversion rate: requires 1,000 monthly visitors to the platform's website
- At 2.5% (un-optimized): requires 4,000 monthly visitors to achieve the same 100 signups

The well-built website generates 100 new SMB clients from 1,000 visitors. The poorly built website needs 4,000 visitors to achieve the same result. Acquiring 3,000 additional monthly visitors through paid channels costs approximately $3,000-$9,000/month at fintech CAC rates. The website pays for itself in traffic cost savings within the first quarter.

### Scenario: Subscription Fintech (B2C, 1,000 subscriber target like Thutsé)
- Target: 1,000 active subscribers
- Subscription fee: $20/month
- Monthly revenue at target: $20,000/month
- Churn rate: 5% monthly
- Net new subscribers needed per month to maintain 1,000: 1,000 x 5% = 50 net new subscribers
- At 10% website conversion: requires 500 monthly website visitors to the signup page
- At 2.5% conversion: requires 2,000 monthly visitors

The conversion-optimized website reduces the traffic required to hit subscriber targets by 75%. For a founder spending on paid social to drive signups, this is a direct cost reduction of 75%.

### Scenario: Trading Platform (retail, education-first)
- Target: 500 active funded accounts
- Average monthly revenue per active account: $80 (blended commissions + subscription)
- Monthly revenue at target: $40,000
- Churn rate (trading platforms): 8-12% monthly (higher than average due to performance sensitivity)
- Net new accounts needed per month: 500 x 10% = 50
- At 10% conversion: requires 500 monthly visitors to the signup page
- At 2.5%: requires 2,000 monthly visitors

## CAC Benchmarks for Fintech

Source: firstpagesage.com Fintech CAC Benchmarks, visora.co, focus-digital.co, finextra.com

- Fintech average CAC (SMB focus): $1,450 per customer
- Enterprise fintech CAC: up to $14,772 per enterprise client
- CAC via paid channels has risen 40-60% between 2023 and 2025 due to increased competition for financial keywords
- Additional KYC/AML compliance costs per acquired user: $20-$30 on top of paid CAC
- At $1,450 CAC and $4,800 LTV: LTV/CAC ratio of 3.3:1 (healthy; the benchmark is 3:1 minimum, 4:1 ideal for fintech)

Implication: Because fintech CAC is high, the website's role in reducing paid traffic dependency is more valuable here than in most other niches. A fintech platform that converts at 10% instead of 2.5% from the same organic traffic effectively cuts its CAC by 75% for organically acquired users.
