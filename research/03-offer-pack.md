## Customer voice: 6 phrases to echo in every DM and page

Verbatim from Sub-task 2. Use these exact words, not rewrites.

1. "I'm the only one selling" -- leads the hero copy and the problem-agitate section.
2. "I hired a rep and it didn't work" -- the bad hire objection addressed in the risk-reversal section.
3. "They won't understand my business" -- the top conversion barrier. Named directly and addressed.
4. "We booked [X] meetings in [Y] days" -- the proof format buyers have been conditioned to expect. Every case study follows this format.
5. "I need someone who can hit the ground running" -- used in the how-it-works section to signal speed.
6. "We need consistent pipeline" -- the aspiration. Used in the hero headline variant and CTA copy.

## Top 3 trust signals to lead with

From Sub-task 5, in priority order:

1. **Quantified case studies, named client, specific number, timeframe.** Not "we increased revenue." Format: "MRR from $X to $Y in Z months" or "22 meetings and $760K pipeline in 120 days." Every site Katleho builds must contain at least one case study in this format. Clients who cannot provide a real number get told to come back when they can -- or the site does not include a case study section.

2. **Clutch/G2 review badge with star rating and review count.** The badge alone is weak. "4.9 stars, 127 reviews" is the proof. For Katleho's own agency page on Upwork or a future Clutch profile: collect 5 verified reviews with specific outcomes in the first 90 days.

3. **Founder video testimonial, 60-90 seconds, with a specific number.** One founder saying "we closed $400K in new revenue after Katleho rebuilt our site" outperforms 10 text reviews. Ask the first 2 clients for a Loom testimonial at the 90-day conversion audit.
