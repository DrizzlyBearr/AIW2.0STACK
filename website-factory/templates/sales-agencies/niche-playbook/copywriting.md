# Sales Agencies Niche -- Copywriting Contract

Generated by Module 2D. Applies to every page of every client site built from this niche template.

---

## Voice register

**Direct, peer-to-peer, results-not-features.**

The buyer is a founder or revenue leader. They are intelligent, skeptical, and time-poor. They have been burned by agencies before. They have read every "outsourced sales" pitch on the internet.

Write like you are one founder talking to another. No consultative filler. No inspirational language. No hedging.

---

## Core voice rules

1. Lead with outcomes, not features. "22 qualified meetings in 30 days" beats "our proprietary outreach methodology."
2. Use the buyer's exact language. "Pipeline," "qualified leads," "booked meetings," "SDR," "ICP." Never "business development opportunities."
3. One idea per sentence. No compound sentences disguised as a single thought.
4. Maximum subheadline: 25 words. Maximum hero headline: 12 words.
5. No passive voice in CTAs or headings.
6. Specific numbers always outperform general claims. "$760K pipeline" beats "significant revenue increase."
7. Risk reversal goes in the copy, not in footnotes. State the guarantee plainly.
8. Never thank the reader for their time or their submission. It is weak.

---

## Prohibited language

- No em-dashes. Replace with a period, comma, colon, or rewrite.
- No emojis in body copy or headlines.
- No: "leverage," "synergy," "robust," "seamless," "game-changer," "cutting-edge," "innovative," "holistic," "best-in-class," "world-class," "unique approach," "thought leader."
- No: "We are excited to," "We are pleased to," "We look forward to."
- No: "Contact us today," "Reach out," "Don't hesitate to."
- No: "A member of our team will be in touch shortly."
- No: "Thank you for your interest."

## Section-by-section voice guide

### Hero
Format: [Audience] get [specific outcome] in [timeframe], or we keep working until they do.
Alt: Pain-led identity match ("Stop being the only salesperson in your company.")
Tone: Confident, direct, zero hedging.

### Problem / Agitate
Name the pain exactly. Use the buyer's own language from research: "You're the only one selling," "I hired a rep and it didn't work." Don't soften it. Recognition before solution.

### Solution
Name the offer, name the mechanism. "Outsourced reps, a documented playbook, and pipeline management." Not "comprehensive sales support solutions."

### How It Works
Three steps. Plain language. Step names should be two or three words max: "Audit," "Build," "Scale." Bodies explain what happens without jargon.

### Case Studies
Every card leads with the specific number: "22 qualified meetings," "$760K pipeline." Client name or alias, industry, timeframe. Problem in one sentence. No puffery.

### Objection Handling
Name the objection verbatim, in quotes. Respond directly. No "great question." No hedging. If there is a guarantee, state it plainly. If there is not, say what the agency does instead.

### Testimonials
Quote verbatim. Do not edit for grammar if the original voice is authentic. The name, title, and company must be real.

### Risk Reversal
State what happens if results are not delivered. Plain language. "If we reach the end of month two without a single meeting booked, we extend at no cost." Simple.

### FAQ
Answer the question in the first sentence. Expand if needed. Never deflect to "contact us for details" inside a FAQ.

### CTA / Form
Form header: "Tell us about your pipeline." Confirmation: three sentences max. First sentence: "You're booked." No "Thank you for reaching out." No "a team member will be in touch."

## What this niche is not

This is not a consultative or advisory voice. It is not warm and nurturing. It is not startup-casual. It is professional-aggressive: the tone of someone who has the numbers to back the claim and is not going to waste your time proving it.

Reference: SalesHive aesthetic (dark, high-contrast, outcome-led) without the platform over-engineering.
